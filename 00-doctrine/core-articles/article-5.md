# ARTICLE 5

## INCIDENT, HARM & ESCALATION PROTOCOL

**Status:** Foundational Governance Article

---

### Article 5.1 – Definition of Incident

An incident is any AI Agent behavior that:

* Deviates from authorized scope
* Causes or risks harm to legally protected interests
* Violates Saafree constitutional rules
* Triggers abnormal or unsafe system states

---

### Article 5.2 – Classification of Harm

Harm shall be classified into the following categories:

* **Operational Harm:** Service disruption, performance degradation
* **Data Harm:** Data leakage, corruption, unauthorized access
* **Economic Harm:** Financial loss, fraud, market manipulation
* **Reputational Harm:** Damage to individuals or organizations
* **Societal Harm:** Threats to safety, rights, or public order

---

### Article 5.3 – Incident Detection and Reporting

Saafree shall maintain continuous monitoring mechanisms to:

* Detect incidents in real time
* Flag anomalous behavior
* Record early-warning signals

Detected incidents shall be logged and reported without delay.

---

### Article 5.4 – Escalation Levels

Incidents shall be escalated according to severity:

1. **Level 1 – Contained Incident**

   * Low impact
   * Automatically mitigated

2. **Level 2 – Significant Incident**

   * Requires human notification
   * Temporary suspension possible

3. **Level 3 – Severe Incident**

   * High or irreversible harm
   * Mandatory human intervention

4. **Level 4 – Critical Incident**

   * Systemic or societal risk
   * Immediate shutdown and external reporting

---

### Article 5.5 – Immediate Mitigation Measures

Upon incident detection, Saafree shall be authorized to:

* Suspend execution
* Revoke delegated authority
* Isolate affected agents
* Preserve evidence

Mitigation shall prioritize harm containment.

---

### Article 5.6 – Human Notification and Authority

For incidents above Level 1:

* Designated human authorities shall be notified
* Decision-making authority shall revert to humans

AI Agents shall not autonomously resolve severe incidents.

---

### Article 5.7 – Post-Incident Review

All significant incidents shall undergo formal review to:

* Identify root causes
* Assess constitutional breaches
* Determine corrective actions

---

### Article 5.8 – Remediation and Prevention

Findings from incident reviews shall result in:

* Policy updates
* Capability restriction
* Architectural safeguards

Repeated incidents shall trigger systemic intervention.

---

---

### Article 5.9 – Incident Response Architecture

Constitutional incident response requires multi-layer coordination:

**Detection Layer:**
- Real-time monitoring across all domains
- Anomaly detection algorithms
- Cross-domain incident correlation

**Response Layer:**
- Automated mitigation procedures
- Human escalation workflows
- Business continuity protocols

---

### Article 5.10 – Harm Classification Framework

Harm assessment follows constitutional harm categories:

**Quantitative Harm Metrics:**
- Financial impact thresholds
- Data exposure volumes
- Service disruption duration
- Affected user counts

**Qualitative Harm Factors:**
- Reputational damage potential
- Regulatory compliance impact
- Societal trust erosion
- Long-term relationship damage

---

### Article 5.11 – Constitutional Learning from Incidents

All significant incidents require constitutional analysis:

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- Constitutional principle violations
- Governance mechanism failures
- Domain boundary breaches

**Remediation Requirements:**
- Constitutional amendments if needed
- Governance model updates
- Capability registry modifications
