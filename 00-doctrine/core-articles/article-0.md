# ARTICLE 0

## RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN NATIONAL LAW AND THE SAAFREE CONSTITUTION

**Status:** Foundational Article

---

### Article 0.1 – Supremacy of Human Law

National constitutions, statutes, and binding international law are supreme over the Saafree Constitution.

Nothing within the Saafree system shall be interpreted as limiting, replacing, or superseding the authority of human law.

---

### Article 0.2 – Non-Personhood of AI Agents

AI Agents governed by Saafree:

* Shall not be recognized as legal persons
* Shall not possess rights, duties, or legal intent
* Shall not bear legal or moral responsibility

All responsibility remains with human or legal persons as defined by applicable law.

---

### Article 0.3 – Mandatory Human Traceability

Every AI Agent action shall be traceable to:

* A responsible human or organization
* A defined authority scope
* An auditable execution context

No anonymous or unaccountable AI execution is permitted.

---

### Article 0.4 – Pre-Legal Enforcement Principle

Where national law defines prohibited or restricted conduct, Saafree shall enforce such restrictions **prior to execution**, whenever technically feasible.

Prevention shall take precedence over post-hoc liability.

---

### Article 0.5 – Jurisdictional Alignment

Saafree shall support jurisdiction-specific rulesets, enabling:

* Local legal compliance
* Cross-border interoperability
* Context-aware enforcement

---

### Article 0.6 – Neutrality and Non-Sovereignty

Saafree shall remain:

* Politically neutral
* Jurisdiction-agnostic
* Non-sovereign

Saafree enforces constraints; it does not define values.

---

### Article 0.7 – Future Constitutional Compatibility

This Article is intended to remain compatible with:

* Future international AI treaties
* Global AI governance institutions
* A potential human-level constitutional framework

Saafree shall evolve to support, not obstruct, such developments.
